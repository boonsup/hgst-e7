# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the HGST E7 open-science project
pledge to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless
of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

**Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and scientific approaches
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism of code *and* physics
- Citing prior work correctly and generously
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the science
- Showing empathy toward other community members

**Examples of unacceptable behaviour:**

- Harassment or discriminatory jokes and language
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Claiming credit for others' simulation runs, proofs, or data
- Submitting intentionally falsified data or manipulated results

## Scientific Integrity

This project makes empirical claims. Contributors are additionally expected to:

- Report all simulation parameters (L, β, κ, seed, N_therm, N_meas) in every
  data PR — no silent changes to canonical values
- Flag potential numerical errors *before* they reach publication
- Not alter committed data files without an accompanying explanation PR

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of
Conduct and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any
behaviour deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the GitHub repository,
Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and any project-related communication.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be
reported to the project lead at **boonsup@kku.ac.th**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the
reporter of an incident.
